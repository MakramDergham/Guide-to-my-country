History

Italy, with a history spanning over millennia, has played a central role in shaping the cultural and political landscape of Europe. From the legendary origins of Rome to the height of the Roman Empire, Italy has been a cradle of civilization. The medieval period saw the rise of powerful city-states, and the Renaissance marked a cultural resurgence. Unification in 1861 brought about a modern nation-state. Italy navigated the challenges of the 20th century, transitioning from monarchy to republic in 1946. Today, Italy stands as a blend of ancient heritage and contemporary influence, renowned for its art, cuisine, and historical significance.

The Etruscans

Around 800BC, the Etruscans arrived in Italy and were skilled craftsmen, metal workers, seafarers, and merchants. They traded with Greeks and Romans, leading to the flourishing of Etruria, a confederation of 12 city-states. They celebrated sacred rites, used divination, and had a hearty appetite for life. The Etruscans influenced Roman art and were praised as "founders of cities."

The Romans

Rome, founded by Romulus in 753BC, was a patrician republic under Etruscan domination. Its aristocracy drew strength from a solid aristocracy. Roman power extended throughout the Mediterranean, winning victories in the Punic Wars and conquests in Macedonia, Asia Minor, Spain, and southern France. Julius Caesar's reformist dictatorship led to civil war and the beginning of the Roman Empire.

The Rise of Florence and the Medici

The Renaissance, a 15th-century art and architecture revolution, began in Florence, a commercial and financial center based on the wool trade. The Medici, influential patrons of art and culture, created magnificent churches, palaces, and works of art. They also established the first public library and Platonic Academy, inspiring artists like Donatello and Michelangelo.

Roman Baroque

The Baroque art movement, originating in Rome from 1600-1750, was influenced by a papal campaign to make Rome a city of beauty. Michelangelo Merisi da Caravaggio was renowned for his realistic portraits and religious paintings, while Gianlorenzo Bernini's St Peter's Church decoration was more accepted.

Italy historical timeline

  • 509 BC
    Founding of the Roman Republic
    Rome becomes a republic, marking the establishment of a republican form of government.
  • 58-48 BC
    Julius Caesar's Conquests and Assassination
    Julius Caesar's conquest of Gaul, crossing the Rubicon, becoming dictator, and his assassination in 44 BC. This period represents a significant turning point in Roman history.
  • 476 AD
    Fall of the Western Roman Empire
    End of the Western Roman Empire, marking the fall of ancient Rome.
  • 1861
    Unification of Italy
    Victor Emmanuel II of Savoy is proclaimed King of Italy, signifying the unification of Italy.
  • 1946
    Birth of the Italian Republic
    Italy is declared a republic after the abdication of King Victor Emmanuel III, marking the end of the monarchy and the establishment of the modern Italian state.
